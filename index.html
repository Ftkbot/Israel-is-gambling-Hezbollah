Israel is gambling Hezbollah will crumple but it faces a well-armed, angry enemy

國際新聞

Israel's leaders are in high spirits about the progress of the offensive against Hezbollah that started with the detonation of weaponised pagers and radios and escalated into intense and deadly airstrikes.

Defence Minister Yoav Gallant did not mince words after Monday's air strikes.

"Today was a tour de force… This was the worst week Hezbollah has had since its establishment, and the results speak volumes."

Gallant said airstrikes obliterated thousands of rockets that could have killed Israeli citizens. In the process Lebanon says Israel killed more than 550 of its citizens, including 50 children. That is almost half Lebanon's casualties in a month of war between Israel and Hezbollah in 2006.

Israel believes that a relentless offensive will compel Hezbollah into doing what it wants, inflicting such severe damage that its leader Hassan Nasrallah and his allies and backers in Iran decide that the price of resistance is exorbitant.

Israel's politicians and generals need a victory. After almost a year of war Gaza has become an imbroglio. Hamas fighters still emerge out of tunnels and ruins to kill and wound Israeli soldiers and are still holding Israeli hostages.

Hamas caught Israel off guard last October. The Israelis did not see Hamas as a significant threat, with catastrophic consequences. Lebanon is different. The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) and the Mossad spy agency have been drawing up plans for the next war against Hezbollah since the last war ended in a deadlock in 2006.

Israel's leader, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, believes the current offensive is making significant headway towards his declared objective of shifting the balance of power away from Hezbollah.

He wants to deter Hezbollah from firing rockets over the border into Israel. At the same time, the Israeli military says the plan is to force Hezbollah back from the border and to neutralize military facilities that threaten Israel.

文章中文翻譯

以色列領導人對針對真主黨的進攻進展感到士氣高昂。這次進攻始於引爆武器化的尋呼機和無線電，隨後升級為密集且致命的空襲。

國防部長尤阿夫·加蘭特在週一的空襲後毫不掩飾他的讚美之詞。

"今天是一次傑出的表現...這是真主黨自成立以來最糟糕的一週，結果不言而喻。"

加蘭特表示，空襲摧毀了數千枚可能殺害以色列公民的火箭。在這個過程中，黎巴嫩稱以色列殺害了550多名黎巴嫩公民，包括50名兒童。這幾乎是2006年以色列與真主黨一個月戰爭中黎巴嫩傷亡人數的一半。

以色列相信，一場無情的進攻將迫使真主黨按其意願行事，造成如此嚴重的損害，以至於其領導人哈桑·納斯魯拉及其在伊朗的盟友和支持者會認定抵抗的代價過於高昂。

以色列的政治家和將軍們需要一場勝利。經過近一年的戰爭，加沙已成為一個複雜的局面。哈馬斯武裝分子仍然從隧道和廢墟中出現，殺害和傷害以色列士兵，並仍然扣押著以色列人質。

去年十月，哈馬斯讓以色列措手不及。以色列人沒有將哈馬斯視為重大威脅，結果造成了災難性的後果。黎巴嫩的情況不同。自2006年上一次戰爭以僵局告終以來，以色列國防軍（IDF）和摩薩德間諜機構一直在為下一場對抗真主黨的戰爭制定計劃。

以色列領導人、總理本雅明·內塔尼亞胡相信，當前的進攻正在朝著他宣布的目標取得重大進展，即改變權力平衡，削弱真主黨。

他希望阻止真主黨向以色列境內發射火箭。同時，以色列軍方表示，計劃是將真主黨從邊境逼退，並消除威脅以色列的軍事設施。
